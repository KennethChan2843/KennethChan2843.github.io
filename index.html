Kenneth Chan

I'm a third year computer science student who was hired as a software developer for NCR from May to December 2019 during my first and second work term. This position allowed me to gain valuable work experience and I learned a lot during these eight months. The following report will provide an overview of my job and detail the learning goals that helped me focus during the work terms.

About NCR

NCR is a leader in omni-channel solutions which enables financial institutions to keep up with new customer experience demands. NCR builds common transaction processing layers between consumer facing channels and core banking systems. With its software, hardware, and portfolio of services, NCR enables nearly 700 million daily transactions across the retail, financial, travel, hospitality, telecom and technology, and small business industries. NCR solutions run the everyday transactions that make your life easier. NCR is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, with over 34,000 employees in 160 countries.

Job Description

As a software developer at NCR, I was assigned to the TxInfinity project where I worked with a team of eight people. My team was responsible for back-end development and I was given a mix of development and DevOps tasks. In order to familiarize myself with NCR's development processes, I learned to use a wide range of tools that I had not used before. These include Jira, Bitbucket, Cucumber, Jenkins, AquaSec, Maven, Docker, Kubernetes, DBeaver, Cassandra, and Postman. In addition, the job required me to write bash scripts, Java code, and code using NCR's proprietary language. After being assigned a Jira story, I was responsible for writing code, test cases, and documentation. Communication and teamwork was essential in order to understand the requirements of a Jira story, create a solid implementation, and to demo the solution to the team and product owners. These eight months were very valuable because it gave me numerous opportunities to develop skills and attempt things that I had never done before.

Goals

For work terms one and two, I created six goals to help me focus on what I wanted to learn and improve on while working at NCR.

Understanding Requirements & Specifications (Term 1)

Starting work was difficult because I did not understand what my team was talking about during meetings. This goal is about understanding the work that NCR does. I need to be familiar with banking terminology in order to have context when requirements and specifications are being discussed. In addition, I need to be familiar with the issue tracking tool, Jira. I would like to explore the different types of stories that are tracked, as well as determine the workload required to complete each type of story. I wish to develop my understanding of story points in order to be able to accurately predict workload and plan sprints.

I now understand most of the acronyms that are used when discussing requirements and specifications during meetings. I also understand the different types of transactions that are possible and the flow of a transaction. I have worked on many Jira stories and I now have experience with development and DevOps related tasks. I've implemented new features, worked on change requests, fixed bugs, created tests cases, and managed build processes and tools. I have a better understanding of the work that we do and I am able to confidently assign story points during meetings.

Processes & Tools Familiarization (Term 1)

I am not familiar with Scrum, nor with many of the tools used by NCR. For this goal, I would like to understand the general workflow and tools used in software development. I need to go through the entire development cycle in order to be exposed to all of the tools that I will be using to complete my tasks. I will learn about the framework and infrastructure which includes Scrum, Jira, and BitBucket. In addition, I'll learn about the development tools such as Maven, Docker, Kubernetes, Postman, DBeaver, and Cassandra. Finally, I'll learn about the testing tools which consists of Cucumber and Jenkins.

Having gone through an entire program increment, I am now accustomed to Scrum. I've participated in program increment planning, standup, sprint planning, backlog grooming, retrospective, and demos. I can use Jira to track issues assigned to me and BitBucket to find source code and create pull requests. I'm familiar with Maven dependencies, building images with Docker, and maintaining my local Kubernetes cluster. I can use Postman to call API endpoints and DBeaver to inspect Cassandra databases. With Cucumber, I am able to create test cases and Jenkins allows me to verify that my solutions do not break anything.

Oral Communication & Teamwork (Term 1)

This is the first time that I have worked with a team which means that this goal focuses on collaborating with my team members. I would like to improve my communication skills and successfully work with team members to solve problems. First, I should get to know my team and be aware of everyone's area of expertise. If I am unsure of what I am suppose to be doing and/or if I have any questions, then I should ask the appropriate team member for help. Before I start implementing my solution, I should review it with others to ensure that I do not waste time with a flawed design.

Having worked with my team for four months, I am not afraid to ask my team for help when I have a problem or question. On the other hand, as I've worked on more Jira stories and expanded my knowledge, others have reached out to me to ask for help. I was also able to help new hires with the onboarding process by explaining and guiding them through the processes and tools that we use. Furthermore, I am comfortable when I am demoing my solution to my team and product owners.

Code Analysis & Quality Assurance (Term 2)

In the second work term, my increase in experience meant that I was able to understand story requirements, specifications, and code more easily. This goal is about applying my knowledge and analytical skills. I should review pull requests and make comments regarding standards, style, and logic. If the pull requests do not have any issues, then I should mark them as approved; otherwise, it should be marked as needing more work. Also, I should contribute to discussions during meetings in order to share my ideas. Having multiple perspectives to review and contribute will ensure that nothing is overlooked.

Since this was my second work term, I had gained a lot more experience and I was able to more effectively contribute to pull request reviews. I have caught logic and syntax errors before the code was merged to the master branch. Something I need to work on is contributing to discussions during meetings. At times, I had trouble asking questions and was unable to provide insight to my team members. This issue is something that I would like to work on in the future.

Identifying Areas of Improvement (Term 2)

As I've gotten familiar with the codebase, I have noticed code that has failed to meet coding standards. This goal is about improving the quality of the existing codebase which includes identifying code that could be cleaned up and made more efficient, removing unused or redundant code, and refactoring code to conform to coding standards. If I was unable to make these changes immediately, then I would talk to my team lead to create a Jira story. A healthy codebase will increase efficiency, productivity, extendibility, and readability because it's easier to navigate, understand, and maintain.

Overall, I'm happy with the outcome of this goal. When I did not understand why code was implemented the way it was, I would ask a co-worker for their perspective to see if I was missing anything. I would fix the issue if it was within my scope or I would bring it to the attention of my team lead. Something that I could do next time is to follow up on the issues that I've identified and see if they have been worked on or resolved.

Personal Organization & Time Management (Term 2)

This learning goal was inspired by a co-worker who recorded his day to day activities in a physical notebook. I wanted to improve my ability to plan and finish Jira stories and keeping a daily journal sounded like a good plan. To begin with, I would write down what I expected to accomplish for the day. As the day progressed, I would record my activities, any issues that I faced, and how I resolved these issues. Finally, I would see if I was able to finish the day's tasks and take note of anything that I should keep in mind for the next day.

This journal has allowed me to look up past issues and recall their solutions. I'm not wasting time trying to fix issues that I have already solved. I can also apply lessons that I've learned from past activities to future solutions. Furthermore, it has been useful for daily standup, especially after the weekend when I need a refresher of what I was doing the week before. The journal has allowed me to keep track of events which helps me finish Jira stories in a timely manner. It has proven to be a useful tool and I would benefit from continuing to use it.

Conclusion

It was my first time in the workplace and these eight months at NCR have been invaluable. The culture is friendly and promotes an environment that allows its employees to grow. From the beginning, the interview was professional and I felt comfortable answering questions. Ever since I met my team, they have been supportive in my goals and education. Also, having twenty co-ops working here made the workplace transition from an academic setting manageable and less intimidating. I had the opportunity to develop my technical and transferable skills, while engaging in recreational activities through ping pong and company events. NCR is a great place to get your feet wet and I would recommend having a work term here.

Acknowledgments

I would like to thank my team for always answering my questions and making my eight-month stay enjoyable. I had a lot of fun playing ping pong and going out on our biweekly team lunches.
